1. Taj Mahal, India

  • A magnificent embodiment of Shah Jahan’s deep affection for his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this mausoleum is one of the seven wonders of the world that features the finest architectural elements and ornate gemstone décor on ivory-white marble.
  • Taj Mahal sits on the banks of River Yamuna and is surrounded by manicured gardens and a longitudinal fountain, both of which are picture-perfect destinations.
  • Known to be a wondrous marvel in the moonlight, Taj Mahal attracts 8 million tourists every year.

2. Great Wall of China, China

  • The Qing wall in itself stands at 8850 km, of which 6259 kilometres is actual wall and the rest is trenches and natural defences.
  • The wall in it's entirety is 21196 km long.
  • Though the entire length of the great wall is not easy to discern, the beginning of it's construction dates back to 5th - 8th century BCE.

3. Machu Pichu, Peru

  • Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set higher more than 7000 feer above sea level in the Andes Mountains in Peru, above the Urubamba River valley.
  • A symbol of the Incan Empire and built around 1450AD, Machu Picchu was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.
  • Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, it is renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views. Its exact former use remains a mystery.

4. The Colosseum Rome, Italy

  • This 2000-year-old oval-shaped amphitheatre in the heart of Rome city is an iconic retention of Imperial Rome and its unique culture.
  • Also known as Flavian Theatre, Colosseum has seen innumerable gladiator contests, mock sea battles, animal hunts and executions since it was built by Emperor Vespasian.
  • Even though only a section remains today, this stone-built venue once had the capacity of over 50,000 and attracts history buffs and tourists alike.

5. Christ the Redeemer Statue, Brazil

  • Rio de Janeiro’s most famous Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ stands 38 meters tall as a colossal protector of the people
  • It was built to commemorate the anniversary century of Brazil’s independence.
  • It is listed as one of the seven wonders of the world for being a symbol of Christianity and showcases a detailed soapstone build.

6. Petra: A rock cut past, Jordan

  • The majestic red stone architecture and a whole city carved out of rock cuts earns Petra the name of "Rose City".
  • Believed to have been established as early as 312 BC, the city later came under Roman rule.
  • The city is Containing marvels such as the narrow yet long Siq, the Treasury, the Temple of Winged Lions, the Great Temple and even a dam, it is a city for enthusiasts of history and marvellous architecture.

7. Chichen Itza,Cancun, Mexico

  • Chichen Itza, meaning “at Itza’s well’s mouth”, is the remainder of Terminal Classic, pre-Columbian period when it was built by the Mayan and Yucatan civilizations.
  • The highlight of this archaeological site is Kukulcan Pyramid with larger pyramids built atop smaller ones. Listed as one of the seven wonders of the world, the monument has one of the most elaborate excavations of the Jaguar throne within.
  • The centre of attraction being the pyramid El Castillo which used to be a place of worship.
